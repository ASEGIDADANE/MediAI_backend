# Diabetes — general education

## Red flags — emergency care

- Confusion, extreme thirst, vomiting, fruity breath, very high blood glucose if you monitor (possible diabetic emergency)
- Foot wound with redness, swelling, fever, or black tissue
- Fainting or seizures

## When to see your clinician soon

- Frequent high or low glucose readings outside your targets
- New numbness, tingling, or foot sores
- Planning pregnancy if you have diabetes

## General information

- Follow your meal plan, activity guidance, and medicines as prescribed.
- Check feet daily for cuts; wear appropriate footwear; seek early care for foot injuries.
- Carry a source of fast sugar if you use insulin or medicines that can cause low glucose.
- Annual eye and kidney screening as recommended by your care team.
