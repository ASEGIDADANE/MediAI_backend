# High blood pressure (hypertension) — education

## Red flags — urgent care

- Blood pressure very high (e.g. ≥180/120 mmHg) with chest pain, severe headache, vision changes, or shortness of breath
- Sudden weakness on one side, slurred speech, or facial droop (stroke signs — emergency)

## When to see your clinician

- First-time elevated readings — confirm with repeated measurements
- Home readings consistently above your clinician’s target
- Side effects from blood pressure medicines (dizziness, swelling, cough)

## General information

- Hypertension often has no symptoms but increases risk of stroke, heart, and kidney disease.
- Lifestyle: lower salt, regular activity as tolerated, healthy weight, limit alcohol, manage stress.
- Take prescribed medicines as directed; do not stop or change doses without your care team.
- Monitor BP at home if your clinician recommends it; bring a log to visits.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot adjust your prescription or interpret a single reading as a diagnosis.
