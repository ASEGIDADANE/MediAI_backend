# Prolonged cough and tuberculosis awareness (education)

## Red flags — seek care urgently

- Coughing blood
- Severe breathlessness or chest pain
- High fever with shaking chills and confusion

## When to visit a health center for evaluation

- Cough lasting more than 2–3 weeks
- Cough with night sweats and unintended weight loss
- Close contact with someone diagnosed with tuberculosis
- HIV or other conditions that weaken immunity — lower threshold to seek testing

## General information

- Tuberculosis is treatable with a long course of medicines prescribed by national programs — early diagnosis protects you and others.
- Sputum tests and chest imaging are done at facilities; this chat cannot perform them.
- Complete the full treatment course if diagnosed; stopping early drives resistance.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot diagnose or rule out TB. Any concern needs facility-based testing.
