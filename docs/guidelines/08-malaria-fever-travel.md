# Malaria and fever after travel (education only)

## Red flags — go to a hospital immediately

- Fever with confusion, seizures, or inability to drink
- Very pale skin, dark urine, or severe weakness with fever
- Pregnancy with fever in a malaria-risk area

## When to get tested at a health facility

- Fever or flu-like illness within weeks of travel to or residence in a malaria-endemic area of Ethiopia or neighboring regions
- Cyclic fevers, chills, and sweats
- Any fever in a child under 5 in endemic areas — seek same-day facility assessment

## General information

- Malaria is diagnosed with a blood test at a clinic — not by symptoms alone.
- Prevention includes bed nets, repellents, and prophylactic medicines when prescribed for travel — follow national guidelines and your clinician.
- Complete the full course if antimalarial treatment is prescribed; do not stop early.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot confirm or rule out malaria. Testing and treatment must be done by licensed clinicians.
