# Allergies and severe allergic reactions

## Red flags — emergency immediately

- Trouble breathing, wheezing, throat tightness after food, medicine, or sting
- Swelling of lips, tongue, or face with dizziness or fainting
- Widespread hives with vomiting or collapse

Use emergency services; if you have an epinephrine auto-injector and were trained to use it, follow your action plan while help is on the way.

## Non-emergency allergy care

- Mild hay fever or skin rash — discuss antihistamines with a pharmacist or clinician
- Document known drug allergies in your health profile and tell every prescriber

## General information

- Food, drug, and environmental allergies vary; testing and management plans are done at facilities.
- If you listed allergies in MediAI personalized chat, the assistant should respect them but cannot replace allergy testing.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot confirm allergy vs intolerance; referral and testing need a clinician.
