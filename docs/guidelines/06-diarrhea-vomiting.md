# Diarrhea and vomiting

## Red flags — urgent care at a hospital

- Blood in vomit or stool (red or black/tarry)
- Signs of severe dehydration: no urine for 8+ hours, sunken eyes, confusion, rapid heartbeat, dizziness when standing
- Severe abdominal pain that is constant and worsening
- High fever with bloody diarrhea
- Vomiting that prevents any fluids for 12+ hours (adults) or any concerning symptoms in infants/children — bring them to a facility

## When to see a clinician soon

- Diarrhea lasting more than 2–3 days in adults
- Recent antibiotic use with watery diarrhea (possible C. difficile — needs assessment)
- Weight loss or persistent symptoms after travel

## General information

- Sip oral rehydration solution (ORS) or clean water frequently; small, frequent sips if vomiting.
- Wash hands; diarrheal illness can spread in households.
- Avoid anti-diarrhea medicines unless a clinician recommends them — they are not always appropriate for infectious causes.
- In Ethiopia, cholera outbreaks can occur in some settings — seek facility care if many people in the area have severe watery diarrhea.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot prescribe antibiotics or IV fluids. Severe dehydration needs facility-based care.
