# Chest pain and breathlessness

## Red flags — call emergency services or go to the nearest hospital immediately

- Crushing or heavy chest pain, especially with sweat, nausea, or pain in the arm, jaw, or back
- Sudden severe shortness of breath at rest
- Chest pain with fainting or near-fainting
- Sudden sharp chest pain with rapid breathing (possible blood clot in lungs — needs emergency care)
- Blue lips or face, inability to speak full sentences due to breathlessness

## When to seek care soon

- New chest discomfort with activity that eases with rest (possible heart-related — needs evaluation)
- Wheezing or breathlessness that is worse than your usual pattern if you have asthma or COPD
- Chest pain after an injury

## General information

- Many conditions can cause chest symptoms, from muscle strain to heart or lung problems. Only a clinician can determine the cause.
- If you have known heart disease, follow your care team’s action plan for new symptoms.
- Avoid driving yourself if symptoms are severe; ask for help to reach a facility.
