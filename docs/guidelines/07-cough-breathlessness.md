# Cough and ongoing breathlessness

## Red flags — seek urgent hospital care

- Coughing up blood (more than streaks)
- Severe breathlessness at rest or lips turning blue
- Chest pain with cough and fever
- Sudden swelling of face or throat with breathing difficulty (possible allergy emergency)

## When to see a clinician soon

- Cough lasting more than 3 weeks
- Unintended weight loss with cough
- Night sweats and fever for more than 2 weeks (needs evaluation for infections including tuberculosis)
- Worsening asthma or COPD symptoms despite usual inhalers

## General information

- Most coughs are viral and improve over 1–2 weeks. Rest, fluids, and honey (if not allergic) may soothe mild cough in adults.
- Smoking worsens cough — consider cessation support through a health program.
- In Ethiopia, prolonged cough should be evaluated at a health center; tuberculosis is treatable when diagnosed early — do not self-diagnose.

## What this chat cannot do

- Cannot order chest X-rays or sputum tests. TB and other diagnoses require facility-based assessment.
