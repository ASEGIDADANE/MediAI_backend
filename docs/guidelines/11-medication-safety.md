# Medication safety

## Red flags — seek urgent care

- Swelling of lips/tongue, hives, or trouble breathing after a new medicine (possible anaphylaxis)
- Overdose or taking someone else’s prescription intentionally or by mistake
- Severe rash with fever after starting an antibiotic

## General principles

- Take medicines only as prescribed; do not share prescriptions.
- Tell clinicians and pharmacists about all medicines, supplements, and herbal products you use.
- Ask about interactions when starting something new.
- Do not stop long-term medicines (blood pressure, diabetes, mental health) without professional guidance — sudden stops can be harmful.
- Store medicines out of reach of children; check expiry dates.

## In Ethiopia

- Buy medicines from licensed pharmacies when possible; ask the pharmacist if instructions are unclear.
- Traditional remedies may interact with prescribed drugs — discuss with your clinician.
